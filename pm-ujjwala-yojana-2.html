The Real Problem This Scheme Solves

Think about what cooking on firewood or dung cakes actually means for a family. It means someone — almost always a woman or a girl — spends 2–4 hours a day collecting fuel. It means kitchens filled with smoke that causes chronic respiratory illness. Studies have consistently shown that indoor air pollution from biomass cooking fuels is one of the leading causes of preventable death among rural women and young children in India.

PM Ujjwala Yojana was launched in 2016 to address exactly this. Phase 1 distributed 8 crore LPG connections to BPL households. When the government evaluated the data, millions of deserving households still hadn't been covered — especially migrants, those without formal address proof, and households in remote areas. That's why Ujjwala 2.0 was launched in August 2021, targeting an additional 1 crore connections with even simpler eligibility rules for migrant families.

This guide is about that second phase — who qualifies in 2025, what you actually receive, and how to apply without confusion or delay.

The ₹1,600 covers the cost of the LPG connection deposit and regulator. This is provided as a loan from the oil marketing company, but for Ujjwala 2.0 beneficiaries, this amount is waived — you don't have to repay it. Plus, your first gas refill and a basic two-burner stove are provided at no cost, so you can start cooking on clean fuel from day one.

✅ Key Benefit
Unlike the initial phase where the ₹1,600 was a recoverable loan, Ujjwala 2.0 beneficiaries do not need to repay this amount. The connection, first refill, and stove are all provided free of charge.

Eligibility: Who Can Apply for Ujjwala 2.0?

The eligibility rules are clear — and for migrant households, Ujjwala 2.0 is significantly more accessible than Phase 1. Here's the complete criteria:

Basic Eligibility Requirements

  • The applicant must be a woman, aged 18 years or above
  • The household must be classified as Below Poverty Line (BPL)
  • There should be no existing LPG connection in the same household
  • The applicant must belong to one of the qualifying categories (see below)

Qualifying Household Categories

Your household must fall into at least one of these categories:

  • SC/ST households
  • PMAY (Gramin) beneficiaries
  • Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) cardholders
  • Most Backward Classes (MBC) households
  • Tea and Ex-Tea Garden Tribes
  • Forest Dwellers
  • Residents of Islands and River Islands

The Migrant Applicant Rule — A Major Upgrade in 2.0

This is perhaps the most important improvement in Ujjwala 2.0. Migrant workers who have moved from their home state to cities often couldn't get gas connections because they lacked formal address proof at their current location. The old rules required a ration card — which many migrants don't have at their new address.

Under Ujjwala 2.0, migrants can now apply with just a self-declaration of their current address and a family undertaking. No ration card required for address proof. This opened the scheme to lakhs of families who were previously stuck.

📌 For Migrant Workers
If you've moved to a different city or state for work and don't have a local ration card, you can still apply under Ujjwala 2.0. A self-written declaration mentioning your current address, along with your Aadhaar card, is sufficient as proof.

Step-by-Step: How to Apply for PM Ujjwala Yojana 2.0

You have two routes — online (faster) or offline through your nearest LPG distributor. Both work. Here's the online process:

  1. Visit the Official Portal Go to pmuy.gov.in. Look for the "Apply for New Ujjwala 2.0 Connection" button on the homepage. Avoid third-party sites that may charge fees — the official portal is completely free.
  2. Choose Your LPG Distributor You'll be asked to choose your preferred LPG provider: Indane (Indian Oil), Bharatgas (Bharat Petroleum), or HP Gas (Hindustan Petroleum). All three are government-owned oil companies. Choose based on availability in your area — usually there's a distributor nearby regardless of which you pick.
  3. Fill the Application Form Complete the form with your personal details — name as on Aadhaar, address, date of birth, and bank account number. If you're a migrant applicant, select the "Migrant" category and fill in your current address in the self-declaration field.
  4. Upload Your Documents Upload scanned copies (or clear phone photographs) of your Aadhaar card, BPL/ration card, bank passbook, and passport-size photo. If you're a migrant, upload your self-declaration instead of a ration card for address proof.
  5. Submit and Note Your Application Number After submission, you'll receive an application reference number. Note it down — you'll need it to track your application status. An LPG field official will typically contact you within 7–14 working days to verify and complete the installation.

Applying Offline (at LPG Distributor Office)

If you're not comfortable with the online process, simply walk into your nearest Indane, Bharatgas, or HP Gas distributor with your documents and ask for the Ujjwala 2.0 application form. They're required to assist you. The process is the same — you fill the form, submit documents, and a field official comes to your home for verification and installation.

⚠️ Warning
Do not pay anyone to process your Ujjwala application. The connection is completely free. If a distributor employee or agent is demanding money, report them to the PMUY helpline immediately.

Connecting Your Subsidy: The PAHAL Scheme

Once you have your Ujjwala 2.0 connection, you should enroll in the PAHAL (Direct Benefit Transfer for LPG) scheme. Under PAHAL, LPG subsidies are credited directly to your bank account. You pay the market rate when buying a refill, and the subsidy difference comes back to your bank account within a day or two.

To enrol, link your LPG consumer number to your Aadhaar and bank account. Your distributor can help with this, or you can do it through the respective oil company's app (IndianOil One, SmartDrive, or HP Gas app). This is an important step — without it, you won't automatically receive the subsidy.

What Happens After You Get the Connection?

Many families receive the Ujjwala connection but then stop using it after the first free refill because they can't afford the subsequent refill costs at market price. This is a real challenge. Here's what you should know:

  • LPG subsidies are available through PAHAL — enroll so each refill is cheaper after the subsidy credit.
  • Some states offer additional state-level subsidies on LPG for BPL households. Check with your distributor whether your state has such a scheme.
  • EMI options are sometimes available through distributors for households that find the full refill cost difficult to pay upfront.

The goal of the scheme isn't just to hand over a cylinder — it's to make clean cooking fuel a permanent habit. Making use of the subsidy mechanism is what makes it financially sustainable for your family.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. Can a woman apply even if her husband already has an LPG connection?
No. One of the core conditions is that no LPG connection should already exist in the same household. If your husband has a registered connection under his name at your current address, you are not eligible for a new Ujjwala connection. However, if the existing connection is at a different address (say, in the village while you live in a city), you may be eligible — consult your LPG distributor for a case-by-case assessment.
Q2. I don't have a BPL card. Can I still apply?
You need proof that your household is BPL. If you don't have a BPL card, an Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) ration card, a state government certificate declaring BPL status, or proof that you are a PMAY-G beneficiary can substitute. Check with your LPG distributor about which documents your state accepts.
Q3. What is the difference between Ujjwala 1.0 and Ujjwala 2.0?
Ujjwala 1.0 (launched 2016) targeted households in the SECC 2011 list and required formal documentation. The ₹1,600 was provided as a recoverable loan. Ujjwala 2.0 (launched 2021) expanded the target to 1 crore additional households, waived the loan requirement (connection is fully free), and crucially allowed migrants to apply with a self-declaration instead of a ration card for address proof.
Q4. How long does the application take to process?
Typically 7–14 working days from submission. An LPG field officer will visit your home to verify your address and install the connection. If you haven't heard back within two weeks of applying online, call the helpline (1800-233-3555) or visit your chosen distributor's office in person with your application number.
Q5. Is Aadhaar mandatory for the application?
Yes, Aadhaar is mandatory as the primary KYC document for both identity and address proof. For migrant applicants, Aadhaar serves as identity proof while the self-declaration covers the address. Aadhaar is also required to link your bank account for the PAHAL subsidy.
Q6. Can I choose any LPG company, or am I assigned one?
You can choose among the three major oil companies — Indane, Bharatgas, or HP Gas. The choice is yours. Once selected, you're assigned a specific distributor from that company in your area. You cannot switch companies after the connection is issued, though you can transfer between distributors within the same company.

Summary — Your Action Checklist

If you or someone in your family qualifies for PM Ujjwala Yojana 2.0, here's what to do:

  • ✅ Confirm you're a woman aged 18+ from a BPL household
  • ✅ Ensure no existing LPG connection is registered at your address
  • ✅ Keep Aadhaar, BPL/ration card, bank passbook, and photo ready
  • ✅ Apply at pmuy.gov.in or visit your nearest Indane/Bharatgas/HP Gas distributor
  • ✅ If you're a migrant, prepare a self-declaration letter for your current address
  • ✅ After receiving the connection, enroll in PAHAL to receive LPG subsidies directly
  • ✅ Helpline for any issues: 1800-233-3555 (toll-free)
