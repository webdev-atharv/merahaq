🖥️ Online Application

Online Application — Step by Step for All States

  1. 1
    Find Your State's PDS PortalEvery state has a separate Food & Civil Supplies portal. See the state-wise links below. For a national gateway, visit nfsa.gov.in.
  2. 2
    Register with Mobile & Aadhaar OTPCreate an account using your Aadhaar-linked mobile number. Verify with OTP. Some states use SSOID or state citizen login.
  3. 3
    Select "New Ration Card Application"From the dashboard, select "Apply for New Ration Card" or equivalent. Choose card type: AAY (for very poor) or PHH/BPL based on your income.
  4. 4
    Fill Family DetailsEnter the head of family's details and add each family member with their Aadhaar numbers, relationship, age, and gender. Double-check names match Aadhaar exactly.
  5. 5
    Upload DocumentsUpload scanned copies of Aadhaar (all members), address proof, income certificate, and photograph. Most portals require files under 200KB in JPEG or PDF format.
  6. 6
    Submit and Save Application NumberSubmit the form. You receive an application reference number by SMS. Save this — use it to track status and for any follow-up.
  7. 7
    Field VerificationA supply inspector visits your home within 15–30 days to verify details. Keep original documents ready. Be cooperative — this visit is mandatory in most states.
  8. 8
    Card Issued / Download e-CardAfter approval (30–60 days typically), collect physical card from supply office or download e-ration card from the state portal. Begin collecting rations from your assigned Fair Price Shop.

❓ FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Under NFSA rules, a ration card must be issued within 30 days of a complete application. In practice, field verification and administrative processing takes 30–60 days in most states. If more than 60 days pass without any response, file a complaint on the state PDS portal or call 1967.
  • In many states, a self-declaration of income is accepted in place of a formal income certificate for BPL applications. Some states also accept an affidavit. If you genuinely can't get an income certificate, contact your local CSC or Gram Panchayat — they can guide you on the alternative accepted in your state.
  • Yes, always. Offline application at the supply office or through a CSC operator is equally valid. Take the form from the Gram Panchayat or Ward Office, fill it, attach documents, and submit with an acknowledgement receipt. CSC centres charge ₹20–50 for this assisted service.
