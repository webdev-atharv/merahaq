📖 Overview

What Is Ujjwala Yojana — And Why It Was Created

Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY) was launched on May 1, 2016, by the Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas. Its core purpose is to provide free LPG (liquefied petroleum gas) connections to women from Below Poverty Line (BPL) households — replacing toxic solid cooking fuels like wood, coal, and cow dung.

Before Ujjwala, over 70% of rural India cooked on biomass fuels. The smoke from these fires — equivalent to smoking 400 cigarettes per hour — was killing approximately 5 lakh Indians every year from respiratory diseases, primarily women and children who spent the most time near the cooking area. Ujjwala was designed to end this silent health crisis.

The scheme provides a free LPG connection in the name of the adult woman of the household — not the husband or any male member. This was a deliberate policy decision to give women control over a household resource and increase their economic agency.

Ujjwala 1.0 vs Ujjwala 2.0 — What Changed

The original Ujjwala scheme (Phase 1, 2016–2020) targeted BPL families identified through the Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) 2011 data. It set a target of 8 crore connections, which was achieved ahead of schedule in September 2019.

Ujjwala 2.0, launched on August 10, 2021, expanded the scheme significantly. The most important new addition: migrant workers no longer need to submit address proof at their current location. A simple self-declaration of their address is now accepted — a major change that previously excluded millions of construction workers, domestic workers, and seasonal migrants who lacked permanent address documents.

💡 Key Difference in Ujjwala 2.0 Under Ujjwala 2.0, the first LPG cylinder refill is also provided free of charge, in addition to the free connection. The beneficiary also receives a free gas stove (hotplate) if they cannot afford one. This removes the most common barrier — people got the connection but couldn't afford the first refill.
✅ Eligibility

Who Is Eligible for Ujjwala Yojana — Complete Criteria

Eligibility has been expanded significantly since Phase 1. Here is the complete current eligibility list:

Eligible Categories (Any One Is Sufficient)

  • SECC 2011 listed households — if your household appeared in the Socio-Economic Caste Census 2011 data, you automatically qualify
  • PM Gramin Awaas Yojana beneficiaries (PMAY-G) — families who received a PMAY rural housing grant
  • Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY) cardholders — families holding an AAY ration card (the yellow/priority ration card for the poorest of the poor)
  • Forest dwellers — most primitive tribal groups and forest-dwelling SC/ST communities
  • MGNREGA Job Card holders — if you have an active MGNREGA job card with at least one day of work in the past year
  • Tea and ex-tea garden tribal families — in Assam and West Bengal
  • River island dwellers — people residing on river islands (char/chapori areas) in Northeast India
  • SC/ST households — all Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe households are eligible regardless of SECC listing
  • States with poor sanitation as per 14th Finance Commission criteria — households in the poorest districts identified by this benchmark

Basic Conditions (Must Meet ALL)

  • The applicant must be an adult woman aged 18 or above — connection is issued in her name only
  • The household must not already have an LPG connection in any family member's name
  • The woman must have a bank account (even a Jan Dhan zero-balance account works)
  • The woman must have an Aadhaar card
⚠️ If There Is Any LPG Connection in Your Home: If your husband, father-in-law, or any family member already has an LPG connection registered at your address, you will not qualify even if you meet all other criteria. The household is treated as already covered. This is a common reason for rejection — check before applying.

🖥️ How to Apply

Step-by-Step Application Process — Online and Offline Both

Method 1: Apply Online (Fastest)

  1. 1
    Visit mylpg.in or pmuy.gov.inGo to the official PMUY portal at pmuy.gov.in or directly at mylpg.in. Click on "Apply for New Ujjwala 2.0 Connection".
  2. 2
    Choose Your LPG CompanySelect from Indane (Indian Oil), HP Gas (HPCL), or Bharat Gas (BPCL) — all three are available and have equal coverage. Choose the one with a distributor nearest to your home.
  3. 3
    Fill the KYC FormEnter your Aadhaar number, full name (exactly as on Aadhaar), date of birth, mobile number, and bank account details. Select your BPL category from the dropdown.
  4. 4
    Upload DocumentsUpload a photo of your Aadhaar card, bank passbook, ration card, and a recent passport-size photograph. File size should be under 2MB each; JPG or PDF format accepted.
  5. 5
    Submit and Note Application IDSubmit the form. You will receive an application reference number via SMS on your Aadhaar-linked or entered mobile number. Note this number — it is needed for tracking.
  6. 6
    Distributor Visit for Physical KYCWithin 2–4 weeks, your nearest LPG distributor will contact you for a physical verification visit. They will confirm your address and collect original document copies. After verification, the connection is activated and the cylinder is delivered to your home.

Method 2: Apply at LPG Distributor (Offline)

Visit any nearest Indane, HP Gas, or Bharat Gas distributor in person. Ask for the Ujjwala 2.0 application form (Form KYC5). Fill it with the help of the distributor staff if needed. Submit along with photocopies of your documents. The distributor processes the application at their end — no online access required from your side.

Method 3: Apply at CSC (Common Service Centre)

Visit any Jan Seva Kendra (Common Service Centre) in your area. CSC operators are authorised to fill and submit Ujjwala applications on your behalf. A nominal fee of ₹20–30 may be charged for the service. CSCs are available in most panchayats and urban wards — find your nearest one at locator.csccloud.in.

As of 2024, the government also provides an additional ₹200 per cylinder subsidy to Ujjwala beneficiaries, making their refill cost significantly lower than market rate.
⚠️ Common Problems

Application Rejected or Cylinder Not Delivered — What to Do

Reason 1: Duplicate Connection Found

If an LPG connection already exists in your household (in any family member's name at your address), your application will be rejected. Solution: Check using the IVRS system — call 1906 (Indane), 1800-233-3555 (HP Gas), or 1800-22-4344 (Bharat Gas) to check connections at your address. If the existing connection belongs to a deceased person or someone who has moved permanently, you can apply for surrender of that connection and then apply fresh under Ujjwala.

Reason 2: Name Mismatch with Aadhaar

If your name on the ration card, bank account, and Aadhaar don't match exactly (even a spelling variation), the application is rejected. Fix the name on whichever document is wrong — ration card corrections can be made at your nearest ration shop or civil supplies office.

Reason 3: Bank Account Not Aadhaar-Linked

The LPG subsidy is transferred via Direct Benefit Transfer, which requires your bank account to be seeded with Aadhaar. Visit your bank branch with your Aadhaar card to link them. This is free and takes 2–3 working days to activate.

Escalation: If Distributor Is Unresponsive

If your application is stuck or the distributor is not responding, escalate by calling the oil company helplines: Indane: 1800-233-3555, HP Gas: 1800-233-3555, Bharat Gas: 1800-22-4344. You can also register a grievance on the Centralized Public Grievance Redress and Monitoring System (CPGRAMS) at pgportal.gov.in.
