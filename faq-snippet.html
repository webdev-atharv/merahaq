Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions About RTI

Can I file an RTI for a state government department? +

Yes. RTI applies to all public authorities — central, state, and local government. For state government departments, you cannot use rtionline.gov.in. Instead, search for your state's RTI portal (e.g., "Maharashtra RTI portal") or submit your application by post/in person to the Public Information Officer of that department.

What if the government does not reply to my RTI within 30 days? +

If you receive no reply within 30 days, you can file a First Appeal with the First Appellate Authority of the same department — this is free and must be done within 30 days of the missed deadline. If the First Appeal also fails, you can file a Second Appeal with the Central Information Commission (CIC) or State Information Commission. They can impose a penalty of ₹250 per day (up to ₹25,000) on the PIO for non-compliance.

Do I need to give a reason for my RTI application? +

No. Under the RTI Act, you are not required to give any reason for requesting information. The government cannot reject your RTI application because you did not explain why you want the information. However, providing context can sometimes help get a more specific response.

What information cannot be obtained through RTI? +

Some information is exempt from disclosure under Section 8 of the RTI Act:

  • Information that would affect national security or sovereignty
  • Cabinet papers and Council of Ministers deliberations
  • Personal information where disclosure would cause unwarranted invasion of privacy
  • Information received in confidence from a foreign government
  • Information that would prejudice ongoing investigations or proceedings

However, even exempt information can be disclosed if public interest outweighs the harm.

Can I file an RTI to know why my PM Kisan / ration card / scheme application was rejected? +

Yes — this is one of the most common and effective uses of RTI. File an application asking for "certified copies of the documents or orders on the basis of which my application for [scheme name] was rejected, along with the grounds for rejection." Address it to the Public Information Officer of the concerned department. They must reply within 30 days with the reason and supporting documents.

Is the RTI process free for BPL card holders? +

Yes. BPL (Below Poverty Line) card holders are completely exempt from the ₹10 application fee and any additional fees for documents. You simply need to attach a photocopy of your BPL ration card with your RTI application. The government cannot charge you any fee if you are a BPL card holder.

Can I write my RTI application in Hindi? +

Yes. You can write your RTI application in Hindi, English, or any official language of the area where the public authority is located. For central government departments, Hindi and English are both fully accepted. For state departments, you can also use the official language of that state.
