🏥 Overview

What Is Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY — And Why It Matters

Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY) is the world's largest government-funded health insurance programme. It provides ₹5 lakh per family per year of cashless hospitalisation coverage at over 28,000 empanelled hospitals across India — completely free, with no premium payment required from the beneficiary.

PM-JAY covers secondary and tertiary care — meaning serious illnesses, major surgeries, chemotherapy, dialysis, ICU stays, and over 1,929 medical procedures. The hospital directly claims reimbursement from the government, so the patient pays nothing — not even a deposit at admission.

Launched in September 2018 under the National Health Protection Mission, PM-JAY has so far authorised over 7.5 crore hospital admissions worth more than ₹90,000 crore — representing families who would otherwise have gone into catastrophic medical debt or forgone treatment entirely.

How PM-JAY Works — Cashless from Day One

When you or a family member needs hospitalisation at an empanelled hospital, here is what happens: You show your Ayushman card (golden card) at the hospital's Ayushman Mitra desk. Your identity is verified against the SECC database using Aadhaar. The hospital pre-authorises the treatment with the government. You receive full treatment — medicines, tests, ICU, surgery — without paying anything. The hospital bills the government directly after discharge.

💡 Portability Across India Your Ayushman card works at any empanelled hospital in any state. If you are a migrant worker in Mumbai but your card is from Uttar Pradesh, you can use it at a Mumbai hospital. Just carry your Aadhaar card and golden card number.
✅ Eligibility

Who Is Eligible — Complete Criteria for 2025

Eligibility is determined by the Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) 2011 database. You do not "apply" for eligibility — your name either appears in the SECC list or it doesn't. Check instantly at pmjay.gov.in or by calling 14555.

Rural Families — Automatically Included Categories

  • Families living in one-room kutcha (mud/thatched) houses
  • Households with no adult male member aged 16–59 years
  • Female-headed households with no adult male earner
  • Households with a disabled member and no able-bodied adult earner
  • SC/ST households
  • Landless labourers dependent on manual casual labour
  • Primitive tribal groups and bonded labourers (automatically included)

Urban Poor — Eligible Occupation Categories

  • Rag-pickers, beggars, street vendors, cobblers, hawkers
  • Construction workers, plumbers, masons, painters, welders
  • Sweepers, sanitation workers, gardeners, security guards
  • Transport workers — drivers, conductors, cart-pullers, rickshaw drivers
  • Home-based artisans, handicraft workers, domestic helpers
  • Electricians, mechanics, assemblers, small repair workers
  • Washermen, watchmen, peons in establishments with 10 or fewer workers
📌 No Income Cap: Unlike most schemes, PM-JAY does not have an income ceiling. If your occupation or household profile matches the SECC criteria above, you are eligible regardless of your current income — as long as you are not employed by the central or state government with a formal job and benefits.

⚠️ What Is NOT Covered: PM-JAY covers only inpatient (hospitalisation requiring at least one overnight stay) treatment. OPD consultations, standalone pharmacy purchases, and medicines outside hospitalisation are not covered. For free OPD care, visit Ayushman Arogya Mandirs (Health & Wellness Centres) near you.
💳 Golden Card

How to Get Your Ayushman Golden Card — Step by Step

  1. 1
    Check Eligibility FirstVisit pmjay.gov.in and click "Am I Eligible". Enter your mobile number for OTP. Search by name, ration card number, or SECC ID. Or call 14555 for assistance.
  2. 2
    Visit a Registration PointIf eligible, go to the nearest Common Service Centre (CSC), government hospital with Ayushman desk, or Jan Seva Kendra. Carry your Aadhaar card and ration card.
  3. 3
    Biometric VerificationYour fingerprint or face is scanned and matched against the Aadhaar UIDAI database to verify identity. All family members listed in the SECC database can be added.
  4. 4
    Card is Printed & Given FreeThe golden card is printed and handed to you on the same day in most cases. There is absolutely no charge for the card — report anyone who asks for payment.
  5. 5
    Alternatively: Download Via AppDownload the official Ayushman Bharat app (Android / iOS) and log in with your Aadhaar to check your eligibility and download your digital card.
💡 No Family Size Cap PM-JAY has no limit on the number of family members covered under one card. All members listed in the SECC database for your household — parents, children, spouse, siblings — are covered under the same ₹5 lakh annual limit which resets every year.
