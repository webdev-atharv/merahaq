🛡️ Overview

What Is the Agnipath Scheme — Complete 2025 Breakdown

The Agnipath scheme, launched in June 2022, is a transformative military recruitment model that allows Indian youth to serve in the Armed Forces (Army, Navy, Air Force) for a period of 4 years as "Agniveers." After 4 years, up to 25% of each batch may be retained for permanent regular cadre; the remaining 75% exit with the Seva Nidhi financial package, skill certificates, and various post-service benefits.

Why the Government Created Agnipath

The scheme aims to: create a younger and more agile military profile, provide defence sector experience to more youth, and develop a large pool of disciplined, trained individuals who can contribute to the civilian economy after service. Agniveers get world-class military training, discipline, and skills that are valued across government and private sectors.

💰 Seva Nidhi & Benefits

Seva Nidhi — The ₹11.71 Lakh Exit Package Explained

The Seva Nidhi is a unique corpus fund built during service:

ComponentAmount
Agniveer's own contribution (30% of monthly package over 4 years)~₹5.02 lakh
Government's matching contribution over 4 years~₹5.02 lakh
Interest accrued at notified rate~₹1.67 lakh
Total Seva Nidhi at exit₹11.71 lakh (approx, tax-free)

Additional Benefits During Service

  • Life insurance: ₹48 lakh cover during 4-year service period
  • Disability compensation: ₹44 lakh for 100% disability attributable to service
  • Death in service: ₹1 crore ex-gratia (₹48 lakh insurance + ₹44 lakh GoI + full Seva Nidhi)
  • Free rations, clothing, and accommodation during service
  • Medical facilities for self during service
  • Annual leave of 30 days and sick leave as applicable

Post-Service Benefits for Exiting Agniveers (75%)

  • 10% reservation in Central Armed Police Forces (CAPF) and Assam Rifles recruitment
  • Priority consideration in state police recruitment (many states have announced reservations)
  • Age relaxation of 3 years in government job recruitment
  • Skill certificates recognised by NSDC for employment in private sector
  • Priority in defence PSUs and Ordnance Factory Board recruitment
  • Credit for higher education — some universities give credits for Agnipath training
