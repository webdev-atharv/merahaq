📖 Overview

What Is a PAN Card — And Why You Absolutely Need One

A Permanent Account Number (PAN) is a 10-character alphanumeric identifier issued by the Income Tax Department. It is mandatory for filing income tax returns, opening a bank account or demat account, investing in mutual funds or stocks, buying/selling property above ₹10 lakh, and hundreds of financial transactions where the government requires identity and tax tracking.

PAN is the primary identifier used by the Indian financial system to track high-value transactions. When you conduct a financial transaction above certain thresholds (typically ₹50,000 for single transactions), your PAN must be quoted. The government uses this data to identify tax non-compliance — which is why getting a PAN, keeping it active, and linking it with Aadhaar is now more critical than ever.

Even if you do not have taxable income, a PAN is essential for: opening savings accounts, applying for credit cards, foreign exchange transactions, investing in fixed deposits above ₹50,000, and buying insurance above certain limits. Getting a PAN is free from fraud and delays when done through official channels.

💡 Instant e-PAN — Free for Aadhaar HoldersIf you have an Aadhaar card with a registered mobile number, you can get an e-PAN instantly (within 10 minutes) completely free of charge at incometax.gov.in. No forms, no documents, no fee. The e-PAN is a PDF with QR code — accepted everywhere as valid PAN. This is the fastest way to get a PAN.
🆕 New PAN Application

How to Apply for a New PAN Card — Three Methods

Method 1: Instant e-PAN (Free — 10 Minutes)

  1. 1
    Visit incometax.gov.inGo to the Income Tax e-Filing portal. Under "Quick Links," click "Instant e-PAN."
  2. 2
    Enter Aadhaar NumberEnter your 12-digit Aadhaar number. An OTP is sent to your Aadhaar-linked mobile number.
  3. 3
    Verify and SubmitEnter the OTP, confirm your Aadhaar details (name, date of birth, address, gender), and submit. Your e-PAN is generated and sent to your email within minutes. Also downloadable from the same portal.
⚠️ Condition: Instant e-PAN only works if your Aadhaar is linked to a mobile number. If your Aadhaar doesn't have a mobile number registered, you must apply through the NSDL/UTIITSL paid route.

Method 2: Online via NSDL (Physical Card Delivered)

Visit NSDL PAN portal. Select Form 49A (for Indian citizens). Fill in name, DOB, address, upload photo, signature, and identity/address proof. Pay ₹66 (+ GST) for delivery within India or ₹959 for international delivery. Physical PAN card is dispatched within 15–20 working days.

Method 3: Via UTIITSL

Visit UTIITSL PAN portal — same process as NSDL with similar fees. Both NSDL and UTIITSL are authorised by the Income Tax Department and provide identical PAN cards.

🔗 Aadhaar-PAN Linking

How to Link Aadhaar with PAN — Step by Step

  1. 1
    Pay the ₹1,000 Penalty FirstGo to incometax.gov.in → e-Pay Tax → Pay ₹1,000 under the specific minor head for Aadhaar-PAN linking penalty. Without this payment, linking will not proceed.
  2. 2
    Wait 4–5 Days After PaymentThe payment must reflect in Income Tax records before linking can proceed. Don't try to link immediately after payment.
  3. 3
    Go to Link Aadhaar SectionOn incometax.gov.in, click "Link Aadhaar" under Quick Links (no login required). Enter your PAN number and Aadhaar number.
  4. 4
    Verify via OTPOTP is sent to your Aadhaar-linked mobile. Enter it to confirm linking. You'll receive a confirmation SMS within 24–48 hours that your PAN is now operative.
❓ FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions — Verified 2025

  • Having two PAN cards is illegal under Section 272B of the Income Tax Act and can result in a ₹10,000 penalty. Surrender the duplicate PAN immediately at the nearest TIN Facilitation Centre (TIN-FC) or submit Form 49A with both PAN numbers and a request to cancel the duplicate. Keep the PAN card you have been using for financial transactions.
  • Apply for a duplicate PAN card via the "Reprint of PAN Card" option on the NSDL or UTIITSL portal. Pay ₹66. You don't need to apply for a new PAN — your old PAN number remains the same. Alternatively, just download your e-PAN from incometax.gov.in — it's free and instant, and is accepted everywhere.
  • A minor can have a PAN card — it is needed if the child has taxable income (like interest income from SSY, FD, etc.) or if the parents want to invest in the child's name. The application is made by the parent on behalf of the minor using Form 49A, with the minor's birth certificate and parent's PAN as supporting documents.
