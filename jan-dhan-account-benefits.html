Jan Dhan Account: 7 Benefits You Are Probably Not Using

Over 53 crore accounts opened — but most holders only use it as a basic bank account. Here’s what you’re actually entitled to.

📅 March 2025 ⏱ 5 min read 🏦 Finance
← Back to Blog

PM Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) accounts are the most widely held bank accounts in India — over 53 crore of them. But most people who have one think of it as just a zero-balance account. In reality, it comes with a package of financial benefits that most account holders are not aware of or not claiming.

Here are all 7 benefits, and how to make sure you are getting each one.

Benefit 1: Zero Minimum Balance

No minimum balance — everUnlike regular savings accounts that charge penalties for low balance, Jan Dhan accounts have absolutely no minimum balance requirement. Your account cannot be penalised or closed for having zero balance.

Benefit 2: Free RuPay Debit Card

Free RuPay Debit CardEvery Jan Dhan account holder gets a free RuPay debit card that works at ATMs and merchant terminals across India. If you haven’t received yours, visit your bank branch and ask for it.

Benefit 3: ₹2 Lakh Accident Insurance

₹2 lakh accident insurance (free)Your RuPay card automatically comes with ₹2 lakh accidental death and disability insurance — completely free. For accounts opened after August 2018, the cover is ₹2 lakh (older accounts have ₹1 lakh cover). There is one condition: the RuPay card must have been used for at least one transaction in the 90 days before the accident.

Benefit 4: ₹30,000 Life Insurance

₹30,000 life insurance (free)Jan Dhan accounts opened between August 2014 and January 2015 came with a free ₹30,000 life insurance cover under LIC. If your account falls in this window and you haven’t checked your cover status, contact LIC or your bank branch.

Benefit 5: ₹10,000 Overdraft Facility

₹10,000 overdraft after 6 monthsAfter maintaining your Jan Dhan account satisfactorily for 6 months, you become eligible for an overdraft (loan) of up to ₹10,000 — without any collateral. This can be a lifeline during emergencies. Contact your bank branch to apply for this facility. Only one member per household is eligible.

Benefit 6: Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT)

Receive all government scheme money directlyYour Jan Dhan account should be linked to your Aadhaar so that all government benefits — PM Kisan, MGNREGA wages, LPG subsidy, scholarship money, pension, and more — are transferred directly to your account. Link your Aadhaar to ensure you receive all DBT payments automatically.

Benefit 7: Interest on Balance

Interest on your savingsJan Dhan accounts earn interest at the same rate as regular savings accounts — currently around 2.75%–3% per year depending on the bank. Even small balances earn interest.
How to open a Jan Dhan account: Walk into any nationalised bank (SBI, PNB, Bank of Baroda, etc.) with your Aadhaar card. Ask specifically for a “PM Jan Dhan Account.” Activation is same-day and your RuPay card will arrive within 10 days.

Quick Summary

  • Zero minimum balance — no penalties ever
  • Free RuPay debit card — ask your bank if you haven’t received it
  • ₹2 lakh accident insurance (use your card at least once every 90 days)
  • ₹10,000 overdraft facility after 6 months
  • Link Aadhaar to receive all government DBT payments
  • Interest earned on all balances
