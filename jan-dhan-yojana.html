What is PM Jan Dhan Yojana?

Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) is India’s national financial inclusion mission launched on 28 August 2014. It gives every Indian household access to a zero-balance savings account along with a RuPay debit card, accident insurance, overdraft facility, and access to all government DBT transfers.

The scheme ensures no citizen is left without a bank account — especially women, rural households, and people who never had access to formal banking before.

Who is Eligible?

  • Any Indian citizen aged 10 years or above
  • No income limit, no caste requirement, no minimum balance
  • Even if you already have a bank account elsewhere, you can open a Jan Dhan account at a different bank
  • Special focus on women, rural households, and first-time bank users

✓ No documents? You can still open a Small Account valid for 12 months using only a self-certified photograph. Upgrade to a full account once you have Aadhaar or any government ID.

How to Open a Jan Dhan Account

  1. Visit any bank branch or Bank Mitra (BC)
    All nationalised banks, regional rural banks, and Bank Mitras in villages accept PMJDY applications. No appointment needed.
  2. Ask for the PMJDY account opening form
    The form is free. Fill in your name, address, Aadhaar number, and nominee details.
  3. Submit your KYC document
    Aadhaar is preferred for same-day processing. Biometric verification may be done on the spot at the branch.
  4. Account activated same day or in 2–3 days
    Your account number and passbook are issued immediately. Your RuPay debit card arrives by post within 10–15 days.
  5. Link Aadhaar for DBT
    After opening, link your Aadhaar to start receiving PM-KISAN, MGNREGA wages, LPG subsidy, and all other government payments directly.

Documents Required

📄 Aadhaar Card (preferred) 📄 Voter ID Card 📄 PAN Card 📄 Driving Licence 📄 NREGA Job Card 📄 Gazette Officer Letter with photo

₹10,000 Overdraft — No Collateral Needed

After operating your Jan Dhan account satisfactorily for 6 months, you become eligible for an overdraft (emergency credit) of up to ₹10,000 — no collateral, no guarantor required.

  • Within first 6 months: overdraft up to ₹2,000
  • After 6 months of satisfactory operation: up to ₹10,000
  • Eligible age: 18–65 years, with Aadhaar-linked account

Insurance Benefits — What You Get

1. RuPay Card Accident Insurance — ₹2 Lakh (Free)
Every Jan Dhan RuPay card automatically carries ₹2 lakh accidental death and permanent disability cover. No premium, no enrolment needed.

2. PMJJBY — ₹2 Lakh Life Cover (₹436/year)
Covers death due to any reason. Auto-debited from your account once a year. Nominee receives ₹2 lakh. Enrol separately at your bank.

3. PMSBY — ₹2 Lakh Accident Cover (₹20/year)
Covers accidental death and permanent disability. Just ₹20 per year. Enrol separately at your bank.

Direct Benefit Transfer — Government Money Directly to You

Your Jan Dhan account linked to Aadhaar is the official channel for all government payments. Once linked, you receive:

  • PM-KISAN — ₹6,000 per year for farmers
  • LPG Pahal subsidy — cooking gas support
  • MGNREGA wages — 100 days of work payment
  • Scholarships, widow pension, disability pension, PM-AWAS

✓ DBT removes all middlemen. The money goes directly from the government to your account — no cuts, no delays, no bribes.

If Account Opening is Refused

  • If a bank refuses to open your Jan Dhan account, file a complaint with the Banking Ombudsman of your state — it is a legal right
  • For DBT issues, call the PFMS helpline: 1800-111-555 (toll-free)
  • For other grievances, use pgportal.gov.in and select the Ministry of Finance
