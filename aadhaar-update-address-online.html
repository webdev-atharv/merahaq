How to Update Your Aadhaar Address Online in 2025 — Complete Guide

Moved to a new city? Update your Aadhaar address from your phone in under 10 minutes. No Aadhaar centre visit required.

📅 March 2025 ⏱ 6 min read 📋 Documents
← Back to Blog

Aadhaar is the foundation of almost every government service, bank account, and official process in India. If your Aadhaar has an old address — especially if you’ve moved cities for work or marriage — it can cause problems with receiving scheme benefits, opening accounts, and linking to other documents.

The good news: UIDAI allows you to update your address online for just ₹50, without visiting any Aadhaar centre.

What Can You Update Online?

  • ✅ Address — online at myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in (₹50 fee)
  • ✅ Mobile number — at any Aadhaar enrolment centre or bank
  • ✅ Email ID — online at myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in
  • ⚠️ Name — must be done at an Aadhaar enrolment centre (cannot be done online)
  • ⚠️ Date of birth — once-in-a-lifetime change, only at centre
  • ⚠️ Biometrics (fingerprints/iris) — only at centre

Online Address Update: Step by Step

  1. Go to myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in
  2. Click “Update Aadhaar” → “Update Demographics Data Online”
  3. Enter your 12-digit Aadhaar number
  4. Enter the OTP sent to your Aadhaar-linked mobile number
  5. Select “Address” as the field you want to update
  6. Enter your new address carefully — match it with your address proof document
  7. Upload a scanned copy of your address proof document (see list below)
  8. Pay ₹50 online (UPI, debit/credit card, net banking)
  9. Note your URN (Update Request Number) to track the status
  10. Update is processed in 5–10 working days

Valid Address Proof Documents

  • Passport
  • Bank statement or passbook (with current address)
  • Post Office account statement
  • Ration card
  • Voter ID
  • Driving licence
  • Government-issued address certificate
  • Utility bill (electricity, water, gas) — not older than 3 months
  • Rent agreement (registered)
  • Property tax receipt
No address proof? If you don’t have any address proof document in your current city, you can use the “Address Validation Letter” service on myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in. A verifier (a family member, friend, or landlord) can digitally confirm your address.

Update Mobile Number (Mandatory for Online Services)

All online Aadhaar services require an OTP on your Aadhaar-linked mobile. If your mobile number has changed, you cannot update it online — you must visit an Aadhaar enrolment centre. There is no fee for mobile number update at official centres.

Find your nearest enrolment centre at appointments.uidai.gov.in — you can also book an appointment online to avoid waiting.

Download Your Updated e-Aadhaar

After your update is processed, download your updated e-Aadhaar (PDF format) for free at myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in. This is a valid Aadhaar document accepted everywhere. You can also order a physical PVC card for ₹50 from the same portal.

Quick Summary

  • Update address online at myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in for ₹50
  • Need your Aadhaar-linked mobile number for OTP
  • Upload any valid address proof document
  • Name/DOB/biometric changes must be done at an enrolment centre
  • Download free e-Aadhaar PDF after update is processed
  • Call 1947 for any Aadhaar-related help
