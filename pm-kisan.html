🌾 Overview

What Is PM-KISAN — And How ₹6,000 Reaches Your Bank

PM-KISAN Samman Nidhi (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi) is a central government scheme launched in February 2019 that provides direct income support of ₹6,000 per year to all landholding farmer families in India. The money is transferred in three equal installments of ₹2,000 every four months — directly into the farmer's bank account through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) with no middlemen involved.

With over 11 crore registered farmers, PM-KISAN is India's largest direct benefit transfer scheme by beneficiary count. Since its launch, over ₹3.24 lakh crore has been transferred in 17+ installments. The money is unconditional — you can spend it on seeds, fertilisers, tools, or any household need.

Why PM-KISAN Matters for Small Farmers

Farming in India is deeply seasonal and uncertain. Small and marginal farmers — who own less than 2 hectares and constitute over 86% of all farmers — often take high-interest informal loans to buy seeds and inputs at the start of each season. PM-KISAN's three installments are timed to coincide with the start of Kharif, Rabi, and Zaid seasons — providing critical cash at the right moment to reduce dependence on moneylenders.

💡 eKYC is Now Mandatory — Do It Immediately All PM-KISAN beneficiaries must complete eKYC to continue receiving installments. Without eKYC, payment is blocked even if you were receiving it earlier. Online eKYC takes 2 minutes at pmkisan.gov.in using your Aadhaar and OTP. Or visit any CSC centre for biometric eKYC — free of charge.
✅ Eligibility

Who Is Eligible — And Who Is Excluded

Who Is Eligible

  • All landholding farmer families — families that own cultivable agricultural land in their name as per land revenue records
  • Both small and marginal farmers AND large landholders are eligible — there is no upper land ceiling
  • Farmers whose land is in the name of any family member (husband, wife, or minor children)
  • Farmers in both rural and urban areas (if cultivable land records exist)

Who Is NOT Eligible — Exclusion List

  • Institutional landholders — companies, trusts, cooperative societies owning farm land
  • Former or current holders of constitutional posts — President, Governor, MP, MLA, Ministers
  • Current or retired government employees receiving monthly pension of ₹10,000 or more (Multi-Tasking Staff, Class IV, Group D employees are exempt)
  • People who paid income tax in the last assessment year
  • Registered professionals — doctors, engineers, lawyers, chartered accountants, architects in active practice
  • Tenant farmers and sharecroppers — those who cultivate land but do not own it are not currently covered
⚠️ Tenant Farmers: If you cultivate someone else's land as a tenant or sharecropper, you do not qualify for PM-KISAN regardless of how long you have farmed. Only the land owner is eligible. Advocacy groups have been requesting inclusion of tenant farmers — check for policy updates.

Common Reasons Installments Are Blocked

  • eKYC not completed — most common reason; do it immediately at pmkisan.gov.in
  • Aadhaar not linked to bank account — link at your bank branch or using NPCI mapper
  • Incorrect bank account details — update via state agriculture department or CSC
  • Land records mismatch — name spelling differs between land record and Aadhaar
  • Name in exclusion list — flagged as income tax payer or government employee
  • Duplicate registration — registered twice with different details
🖥️ Registration

How to Register for PM-KISAN — New Farmers Step by Step

  1. 1
    Visit pmkisan.gov.inOpen pmkisan.gov.in on your phone or computer. Click "Farmer's Corner" then "New Farmer Registration".
  2. 2
    Select Rural or UrbanChoose whether you are a rural farmer or urban farmer (if cultivating land in a notified urban area).
  3. 3
    Enter Aadhaar and MobileEnter your 12-digit Aadhaar number, state name, and Aadhaar-linked mobile number. Verify with OTP.
  4. 4
    Fill Personal DetailsEnter your full name (as on Aadhaar), date of birth, category (General/SC/ST/OBC), and bank account details including IFSC code.
  5. 5
    Enter Land Ownership DetailsEnter your survey number, khata number (from land record/jamabandi), and area of cultivable land in hectares.
  6. 6
    Submit & TrackSubmit the form and note your registration reference number. State government verifies land records in 30–60 days. Once approved, the next installment will automatically be credited.
