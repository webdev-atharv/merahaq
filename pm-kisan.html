PM-KISAN Samman Nidhi
₹6,000 Per Year Directly to Farmers

PM-KISAN Samman Nidhi is a central government scheme that provides income support of ₹6,000 per year to all landholding farmer families in India. The money is paid in three equal installments of ₹2,000 every four months — directly into the farmer's bank account through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) with no middlemen involved. This guide covers registration, eKYC, status check, and everything you need to know about PM-KISAN in 2025.

What is PM-KISAN and Who Started It?

PM-KISAN (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi) was launched in February 2019 by the Government of India to provide income support to the farming community. With over 11 crore registered farmers, it is one of India's largest direct benefit transfer schemes. Since launch, over ₹2.8 lakh crore has been transferred to farmers' bank accounts.

The scheme recognizes that farming is a seasonal and uncertain income, and small/marginal farmers need financial support for agricultural inputs, seeds, fertilizers, and daily needs. The ₹6,000 per year — though modest — arrives three times a year and is completely free of any repayment obligation.

Who is Eligible for PM-KISAN?

All landholding farmer families are eligible — meaning families that own cultivable agricultural land in their name. This includes small and marginal farmers as well as large landholders.

Who is NOT eligible (exclusion list):

  • Institutional landholders (companies, trusts owning farm land)
  • Former/current constitutional post holders (President, Governor, MP, MLA, Minister)
  • Current/retired government employees receiving a monthly pension of ₹10,000+
  • Income tax payees (who filed ITR in the last assessment year)
  • Professionals like doctors, engineers, lawyers, CAs with active professional practice
  • Retired pensioners receiving ₹10,000+/month (except multi-tasking staff/class IV/group D employees)

How to Register for PM-KISAN (New Registration)

  1. Visit pmkisan.gov.in on your phone or computer
  2. Click "Farmer's Corner" → "New Farmer Registration"
  3. Select Rural or Urban farmer category
  4. Enter your Aadhaar number, state, and mobile number → verify with OTP
  5. Fill in personal details: name, DOB, category (General/SC/ST/OBC)
  6. Enter bank account details (account number and IFSC code)
  7. Enter land ownership details — survey number, khata number, land area
  8. Submit — state government will verify and approve your registration

After submission, you receive a registration reference number. Keep this safe to track your application. State verification typically takes 30–60 days. Once approved, you become a PM-KISAN beneficiary and the next installment will be credited automatically.

How to Complete PM-KISAN eKYC

eKYC is now mandatory for all PM-KISAN beneficiaries to continue receiving installments. Without eKYC, your payment will be stopped even if you were a beneficiary earlier.

  1. OTP-based eKYC (online): Visit pmkisan.gov.in → eKYC → Enter Aadhaar number → OTP to registered mobile → Done in 2 minutes
  2. Biometric eKYC (offline): Visit nearest Common Service Centre (CSC) with Aadhaar card. Biometric fingerprint scan confirms identity — free service
  3. Face Authentication: Use mAadhaar or PM-KISAN mobile app for face-based eKYC without visiting CSC

How to Check PM-KISAN Payment Status

Check whether your installment has been released or is pending in just a few seconds:

  • Visit pmkisan.gov.in → Farmer's Corner → "Beneficiary Status"
  • Enter your Aadhaar number, account number, or mobile number
  • View all past installments received, pending installments, and payment dates
  • Also available on the PM-KISAN mobile app (Google Play)
  • Call helpline 155261 or 1800-115-526 (toll-free) for status inquiry
⚠️ Common reasons installments are stopped: eKYC not done, Aadhaar not linked to bank account, bank account details incorrect, land records mismatch, or name in the exclusion list. Check all these if payment stops.

Frequently Asked Questions about PM-KISAN Samman Nidhi

Answers to the most common questions — verified and updated 2026

What if PM-KISAN installment is not received? +
Check: (1) eKYC is done, (2) Aadhaar linked to bank account, (3) bank details are correct, (4) you are not in exclusion list. Contact your agriculture office or call 155261.
Can tenant or sharecropper farmers get PM-KISAN? +
No. Only land-owning farmers are eligible. Tenant farmers, sharecroppers, and landless agricultural laborers are not covered under current PM-KISAN rules.
How to do PM-KISAN eKYC? +
Visit pmkisan.gov.in → eKYC → Enter Aadhaar + OTP. Or visit any CSC center with Aadhaar for biometric eKYC. Both are free of cost.
How to update bank account in PM-KISAN? +
Visit your State Agriculture Department or CSC center with new bank account details and Aadhaar card. Cannot be done online by farmer directly.
Is PM-KISAN money taxable? +
PM-KISAN income of ₹6,000/year is generally not taxable for farmers whose total income (including agricultural income) is below the basic exemption limit.
What is PM-KISAN helpline? +
Call 155261 or 1800-115-526 (toll-free) for registration help, status inquiry, and complaints. Also email at pmkisan-ict@gov.in.
How to check if name is in PM-KISAN exclusion list? +
There is no public exclusion list. If payment was stopped after previous payments, your details may have been flagged. Contact district agriculture office for clarification.
Can PM-KISAN and MGNREGA wages both be received? +
Yes, both are completely separate schemes. A farmer household can receive PM-KISAN installments AND MGNREGA wages simultaneously with no conflict.
