📋 Overview

What Is a Ration Card — And Why Every Eligible Family Must Have One

A Ration Card is an official document issued by state Food & Civil Supplies departments that entitles a household to purchase subsidised or free food grains from Fair Price Shops (FPS) — commonly called "ration shops" — under the Public Distribution System (PDS). The entire system is governed by the National Food Security Act (NFSA), 2013.

Beyond subsidised food, a ration card is one of the most widely accepted identity and address proof documents in India. Banks, courts, schools, and government departments accept it. It is also a key document to verify eligibility for Ayushman Bharat health insurance, PM Awas Yojana housing, Ujjwala LPG connection, and dozens of other central and state welfare schemes.

As of 2026, approximately 81 crore individuals across 23.4 crore households are covered under NFSA. If your household is eligible but does not yet have a ration card, your family may be missing substantial food security benefits every single month.

How the Public Distribution System (PDS) Works

The government procures wheat and rice from farmers through the Food Corporation of India (FCI) and stores it in government warehouses across the country. Every month, allocated quantities are sent to state governments, who distribute through a network of about 5.3 lakh Fair Price Shops. Your ration card determines which shop you are assigned to, how much you can collect, and at what price.

💡 One Nation One Ration Card (ONORC) — Collect From Any State Since 2021, all NFSA ration card holders can collect their monthly ration from any Fair Price Shop anywhere in India — not just their home state. Just carry your Aadhaar card and ration card number. This is especially useful for migrant workers and students living away from home.
🗂️ Card Types

3 Types of Ration Cards — Know Exactly Which One Applies to You

The type of ration card issued determines how much food grain you receive each month and at what price. There are three main categories under NFSA:

AAY · Antyodaya
Antyodaya Anna Yojana Card
35 kg/month
Wheat ₹2/kg · Rice ₹3/kg · FREE under PMGKAY

For the poorest of the poor. A fixed 35 kg allocation per family regardless of how many members. Issued to daily wage earners, landless labourers, rural artisans earning below ₹15,000/year, elderly and disabled persons with no support.

PHH · Priority Household
Priority Household (BPL) Card
5 kg/person/month
FREE under PMGKAY 2024–2028

For families below the poverty line. Allocation is per-person — so a family of 5 gets 25 kg/month. Based on SECC 2011 data and state BPL surveys. Covers landless labourers, small farmers, and urban poor in specific occupations.

APL · Above Poverty Line
Above Poverty Line Card
State decides
Near-market rate · varies by state

For families above the poverty line but not under NFSA entitlements. Primarily useful as an official identity and address document. Many states have reduced or removed APL food subsidies, though the card is still valid ID proof for other services.

⚠️ State Name Variations: Some states use different colour or name systems. Kerala uses White Card (AAY), Yellow Card (BPL), and Blue Card (APL). Maharashtra uses Orange Card (BPL) and Yellow Card (AAY). The core NFSA entitlements remain the same nationwide regardless of colour or local name.
✅ Eligibility

Complete Eligibility Criteria — Who Qualifies in 2026

Who Qualifies for AAY (Antyodaya) Card

  • Landless agricultural labourers and marginal farmers earning below ₹15,000/year
  • Rural artisans — potters, weavers, blacksmiths, carpenters with very low income
  • Slum dwellers with no regular income source
  • Daily wage earners in urban areas — construction workers, rag-pickers, rickshaw pullers
  • Widows, elderly persons above 60, and disabled persons with no family support or pension
  • Primitive tribal groups — automatically included by state governments
  • Released bonded labourers — automatically eligible without application

Who Qualifies for Priority Household (BPL) Card

  • Families whose names appear in the SECC (Socio-Economic Caste Census) 2011 BPL database
  • SC/ST households — automatically included in NFSA coverage in most states
  • Families with income below the state poverty line threshold (varies by state)
  • Urban poor: street vendors, domestic workers, construction labourers, and small traders
  • Female-headed households with no adult male member aged 16–59
  • Families with a disabled member and no able-bodied earning adult

Who Is NOT Eligible — Exclusion Criteria Under NFSA

  • Families who paid income tax in the previous assessment year
  • Government employees or pensioners receiving salary or pension above ₹10,000/month
  • Households owning motorised vehicles (tractors and farm equipment are excluded for farmers)
  • Households with air conditioners, refrigerators, or washing machines
  • Families with irrigated landholding above 2.5 acres (varies by state)
  • Professionals — doctors, engineers, lawyers, chartered accountants in active practice
📌 Note: State governments have flexibility to add or modify inclusion and exclusion criteria beyond NFSA national guidelines. Always verify current eligibility with your state's Food & Civil Supplies department or call 1967.

🖊️ How to Apply

Step-by-Step Application — Online & Offline Both Explained

Online Method (Available in Most States)

  1. 1
    Visit Your State PDS PortalGo to your state's Food & Civil Supplies website. For national links, visit nfsa.gov.in and find your state's link.
  2. 2
    Register / Log InEnter your Aadhaar-linked mobile number and verify with OTP. Some states use direct Aadhaar login.
  3. 3
    Fill the Application FormEnter head of family details, all family members' names, Aadhaar numbers, income details, and preferred Fair Price Shop location near your address.
  4. 4
    Upload Required DocumentsScan and upload Aadhaar cards of all members, income certificate, address proof, and photographs. Files should typically be under 200KB each.
  5. 5
    Submit and Save Your Application IDAfter submission you receive a reference number. Save it to track your application status online or via helpline.
  6. 6
    Field VerificationA supply inspector may visit your home within 15–30 days. Keep original documents ready and be available.
  7. 7
    Collect Your Card or Download e-Ration CardAfter approval (usually 30–45 days), collect the card from the designated supply office or download the digital e-ration card from the state PDS portal.

Offline Method — Via Gram Panchayat or CSC

  • Obtain the form from your Gram Panchayat / Urban Ward Office or a Common Service Centre (CSC)
  • Fill the form in your regional language — CSC operators will assist you
  • Attach self-attested copies of all required documents
  • Submit at the supply office or CSC — CSC charges a nominal ₹20–50 fee for the service
  • Collect your acknowledgement receipt with application tracking number

How to Check Ration Card Status and Download Your Card

  • Official portal: Visit your state PDS portal → "Application Status" → enter your application reference number or Aadhaar number
  • UMANG App: Download UMANG on Android or iPhone → search "Ration Card" → check status using Aadhaar or application reference

⚠️ Grievances

What To Do If Rejected, Denied, or Cheated — Your Complete Legal Options

Millions of eligible families face wrongful rejection or denial of rations every year. You have legal rights under NFSA — here is exactly what to do, step by step:

Step 1: Get the Rejection Reason in Writing

You are legally entitled to know why your application was rejected. Submit a written request to the supply office. If they do not respond within 30 days, file an RTI application (cost: ₹10) asking for the reason. Use MeraHaq's free RTI tool to generate your letter.

Step 2: File a Grievance on the State PDS Portal

Every state PDS portal has an online grievance section. Enter your application number, describe the complaint, and attach supporting documents. You should receive a resolution within 30 days under NFSA guidelines.

Step 3: Approach the District Grievance Redressal Officer (DGRO)

Under NFSA, every district must have a Grievance Redressal Officer. File a written complaint at your district supply office. The DGRO must resolve your complaint within 30 days or provide reasons for the delay in writing.

Step 4: Call National Helpline 1967

The national PDS toll-free helpline handles complaints about card rejections, ration shop denial, short weighing, and quality issues. Available in Hindi and regional languages. Call 1967.

⚠️ FPS Dealer Demanding Bribe or Giving Short Weight? If your ration shop dealer demands money beyond the official rate, gives less grain than your entitlement, or sells poor quality produce — call 1967 immediately. Under NFSA, food grains must be provided at the official rate (currently ₹0 under PMGKAY) in full quantity. Dealers who cheat face suspension and criminal action.
