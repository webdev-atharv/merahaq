Why the National Scholarship Portal Matters

The National Scholarship Portal (NSP), hosted at scholarships.gov.in, is the Government of India's single platform for applying to more than 120 central and state government scholarship schemes. It covers students from Class 9 all the way to PhD level — across SC, ST, OBC, minority communities, persons with disabilities, and general merit categories.

The scholarships are not symbolic amounts. Post-matric scholarship for SC/ST students can cover full tuition fees plus a maintenance allowance. The AICTE Pragati scheme gives women in technical education ₹50,000 per year. The Merit-cum-Means scholarship for minority students can reach ₹30,000 a year for professional courses. This is real money that changes whether a student can afford college or not.

Yet the common complaint among students and parents is: "We didn't know about it." Or: "We applied but got rejected and don't know why." This guide is designed to eliminate both problems.

Who Can Apply?

The NSP covers students across a wide range of categories. Check which group applies to you — you may qualify under more than one:

SC and ST Students

Pre-Matric Scholarship (Class 9–10) and Post-Matric Scholarship (Class 11 and above, including college and professional degrees) from the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment (for SC) and Ministry of Tribal Affairs (for ST). Post-matric scholarships cover full tuition plus a maintenance allowance — this is one of the most substantial schemes on NSP.

OBC Students

Pre-matric and post-matric scholarships are available for students whose parental income is below ₹2.5 lakh per year. The coverage includes tuition, reader charges for blind students, study tour charges for select courses, and a maintenance allowance for hostel students.

Minority Students

Three schemes from the Ministry of Minority Affairs cover Muslim, Christian, Sikh, Buddhist, Jain, and Parsi community students: Pre-Matric, Post-Matric, and Merit-cum-Means scholarships. Merit-cum-Means is particularly valuable for students in technical and professional courses — it covers both tuition and maintenance costs up to ₹30,000 a year.

Disabled Students

The Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities (DEPwD) offers scholarships at both pre-matric and post-matric levels. These cover tuition fees, transport allowance, reader allowance (for visually impaired students), and a general maintenance amount.

Girl Students in Technical Education

AICTE runs two flagship schemes: Pragati (₹50,000/year for women pursuing technical degrees or diplomas) and Saksham (₹50,000/year for disabled students in AICTE-approved institutions). These are among the highest-value scholarships on NSP for the eligible group.

General Merit Students

The Central Sector Scheme of Scholarships (CSSS) is for students from non-reserved categories who scored above the 80th percentile in their Class 12 board exams and whose family income is below ₹4.5 lakh. ₹10,000–12,000 per year for undergraduate students, ₹20,000 for postgraduate students.

⚠️ Critical Deadline Alert
July – November

NSP applications open in July–August and close by November each year. Many students miss the deadline by just a few days. Register as early as July — even if you complete the application later. You cannot apply after the portal closes, and there are no extensions.

How to Register and Apply — Step by Step

The entire process happens at scholarships.gov.in. Here is the step-by-step process for fresh applicants (students applying for the first time):

  1. Go to scholarships.gov.in and click "New Registration" On the homepage, select "Applicant Corner" → "New Registration". Choose "Student" as your applicant type. Read the registration guidelines carefully before proceeding.
  2. Fill in your basic details and select your state and scholarship Enter your name (exactly as in Aadhaar), date of birth, mobile number, email, and select your domicile state. Then choose the scholarship scheme you are applying for from the dropdown list.
  3. Aadhaar verification — OTP on your registered mobile Enter your 12-digit Aadhaar number. An OTP is sent to your Aadhaar-linked mobile number. Enter the OTP to complete verification. If your Aadhaar mobile number has changed, update it at an Aadhaar centre before applying.
  4. Create a username and password — save these safely After Aadhaar verification, set your login credentials. Write them down somewhere secure — you will need them every year for renewal.
  5. Log in and complete the full application form Fill in academic details, bank account information, caste/income details, and institute details. Your institution must be registered on NSP — check this before starting. Incomplete forms are automatically rejected.
  6. Upload all required documents Upload scanned copies of each document (JPG or PDF, within the size limit). Blurry, cut-off, or illegible scans are the second most common reason for rejection.
  7. Submit — note your Application ID After submission, your application goes to your institution for verification, then to the state/district nodal officer, and finally to the ministry. Track its progress at every stage using your Application ID.

✅ After submission: Keep checking your application status on the portal. If it is stuck at "Institute Verification Pending" for more than 2 weeks, contact your college's scholarship coordinator or nodal officer personally. Many applications lapse because the institution doesn't forward them in time.

Documents Required

Have these ready before you start — uploading wrong or outdated documents is the most common reason applications are delayed or rejected.

📄 Aadhaar Card 📄 Class 10/12 Marksheet 📄 Current Year Fee Receipt 📄 Admission Letter / Bonafide Certificate 📄 Caste Certificate (SC/ST/OBC) 📄 Income Certificate (Parent/Guardian) 📄 Bank Passbook (Student's Own Account) 📄 Disability Certificate (if applicable) 📄 Community/Minority Certificate 📄 Passport Size Photo

⚠️ Bank account must be in the student's own name and linked to the student's Aadhaar. Scholarship money is transferred directly to the student — accounts in parents' names are system-rejected. Open a bank account in your own name before applying.

Annual Renewal — It's Not Automatic

This is the point where many scholarship holders lose their benefit after the first year — they assume the scholarship continues automatically. It does not. NSP scholarships must be renewed every year.

Log in to your existing NSP account before the renewal deadline (usually October–November). Update your current academic details, upload your latest marksheet and current fee receipt, and submit the renewal application. Missing the renewal deadline means losing the scholarship for that academic year entirely — there is no grace period.

What you need for renewal

  • Your existing NSP login (username and password from registration)
  • Latest marksheet / result showing you passed and progressed to the next year
  • Current year's fee receipt from your institution
  • Updated bank details if your account has changed

💡 Set a reminder every July to check when NSP renewal opens. Don't wait for your college to notify you — many institutions forget to inform students about the renewal deadline. Your scholarship, your responsibility.

Common Mistakes That Get Applications Rejected

These are the real reasons applications fail — not obscure technicalities, but avoidable errors that students repeat year after year:

  • ❌
    Bank account in parent's name: The system automatically rejects this. Open a bank account in your own name — Jan Dhan, or any nationalised bank account will work. Link it to your Aadhaar.
  • ❌
    Aadhaar name doesn't match other documents: If your Aadhaar says "Rahul Kumar" but your marksheet says "Rahul Kumar Singh", the system may flag a mismatch. Correct your Aadhaar or get a certificate explaining the discrepancy.
  • ❌
    Institution not registered on NSP: If your college or school is not on NSP, your application cannot go through. Push your institution to register — it's their responsibility under government guidelines.
  • ❌
    Applying too late: Some schemes close before the main NSP deadline shown on the homepage. Always check the specific scheme's deadline, not just the general portal deadline.
  • ❌
    Blurry or incomplete document uploads: Scan documents clearly, ensure all four corners of the document are visible, and check that text is legible before uploading. Use a mobile scanning app if needed.
  • ❌
    Not tracking the application: Submitting and forgetting is a mistake. Log in every week and check if your application is stuck at Institute Verification or District level — follow up proactively.
  • ❌
    Not renewing every year: Fresh application and renewal are different processes. After the first year, you must log in and renew — it doesn't carry over automatically.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can apply on the National Scholarship Portal? ›
Students from Class 9 to PhD level belonging to SC, ST, OBC, minority, or disabled categories. General category students who topped Class 12 (above 80th percentile) and have a family income below ₹4.5 lakh can apply for the Central Sector Scheme. Girl students in AICTE-approved technical courses can apply for Pragati regardless of caste.
When does NSP 2025–26 open and what is the last date? ›
NSP typically opens for fresh applications in July–August each year and closes in November. The exact dates vary by scheme — some close earlier. Check scholarships.gov.in in July for the latest schedule and apply as soon as the portal opens. Do not wait until October.
What is the income limit for NSP scholarships? ›
Income limits vary by scheme. For most SC/ST/OBC post-matric scholarships, the family income limit is ₹2.5 lakh per year. For Merit-cum-Means (Minority), it is ₹2.5 lakh. For the Central Sector Scheme (general merit), it is ₹4.5 lakh. Check the specific scheme's terms on the portal.
Can I apply for multiple scholarships on NSP? ›
You can apply for multiple schemes, but most scholarship rules prohibit simultaneously receiving two central government scholarships. The system may automatically block duplicate applications. Apply for the scheme with the highest benefit relevant to your category, or check individual scheme terms.
What happens if my NSP application is rejected? ›
Log in and check your application status for the rejection reason. Common reasons: bank account in parent's name, Aadhaar name mismatch, institution not registered, or missing income certificate. Fix the specific issue and reapply within the window if it's still open. Contact your institution's nodal officer for help with Institute-level rejections.
My institution is not listed on NSP — what should I do? ›
Your institution must be registered on NSP for you to apply. Ask your principal or administrative office to register the institution using their AISHE code. This is their obligation under government guidelines. If they delay, escalate to your state scholarship nodal officer — contact details are available on the NSP portal.
