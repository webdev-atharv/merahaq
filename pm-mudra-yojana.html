📖 Overview

What Is PM Mudra Yojana — And Who It Is Built For

Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY) was launched on April 8, 2015. Its full name — Micro Units Development and Refinance Agency — reflects its purpose: providing institutional credit to the 5.77 crore small businesses in India that are outside the formal banking system and traditionally had to rely on moneylenders at interest rates of 36–60% per year.

Mudra loans target the non-farm micro-enterprise sector: vegetable vendors, small manufacturers, beauticians, tailors, repair shop owners, artisans, small traders, food stall operators, truck and taxi drivers, and anyone running or starting a small income-generating business. The key feature is no collateral requirement — you do not need to pledge property, gold, or any asset to get a Mudra loan.

Mudra is not a direct loan from the government. The Mudra (MUDRA Ltd.) body refinances loans given by member lending institutions — scheduled commercial banks, regional rural banks (RRBs), microfinance institutions (MFIs), Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs), and Small Finance Banks. As a borrower, you apply at one of these institutions, which lends you the money under the Mudra scheme guidelines.

💡 Mudra Is Not a Subsidy — It Is a Loan Mudra loans must be repaid with interest. There is no subsidy element in standard Mudra loans (unlike PM SVANidhi for street vendors, which has an interest subsidy). The benefit is access to formal credit at regulated bank interest rates (typically 10–14% per year) instead of informal moneylender rates (36–60%).

Interest Rates — What to Actually Expect

Mudra does not fix a single interest rate — each lending institution sets its own rate within RBI guidelines. Typical rates in 2024–25:

  • Public sector banks: 8.5% – 12% per year (lowest rates)
  • Regional Rural Banks (RRBs): 9% – 13% per year
  • Small Finance Banks: 13% – 18% per year
  • NBFCs and MFIs: 16% – 24% per year (highest, but most accessible for those without credit history)
⚠️ Always Compare Before Borrowing: The interest rate difference between a public sector bank (10%) and an NBFC (22%) on a ₹3 lakh loan over 3 years is approximately ₹36,000 in additional interest payments. Always apply at a PSU bank first. If rejected, try RRBs and Small Finance Banks before going to NBFCs.
✅ Eligibility

Who Can Apply for a Mudra Loan — Eligibility Criteria

Mudra loans are available to a very wide range of applicants. The main criteria:

  • Indian citizen, 18 years or older
  • Running or planning to start a non-farm micro/small enterprise — trading, manufacturing, or service sector
  • Business should be in the non-corporate sector — proprietorships, partnerships, and small companies qualify; large corporates do not
  • No existing loan default — you must not be a wilful defaulter on any existing bank loan
  • Valid Aadhaar card and PAN card (for Kishore and Tarun categories)
  • A bank account (Jan Dhan account is acceptable for Shishu category)

Who Is Particularly Targeted (Priority Applicants)

While Mudra is open to all, certain categories get preferential processing and in some cases, dedicated loan camps:

  • SC/ST and OBC entrepreneurs — scheduled for priority processing in all PSU banks
  • Women entrepreneurs — some banks offer a 0.25% lower interest rate for women borrowers under Mudra
  • North-East and Hill state residents — special camp-based Mudra disbursements organised periodically
  • Street vendors — covered under both Mudra and the dedicated PM SVANidhi scheme (which also has interest subsidy)

🖥️ How to Apply

Step-by-Step Application Process — 2025

  1. 1
    Decide Your Loan Amount and CategoryAssess how much you need and whether it falls under Shishu, Kishore, or Tarun. Be realistic — borrowing more than you need increases your EMI burden without business benefit.
  2. 2
    Apply Online via Udyamimitra PortalThe fastest way: visit udyamimitra.in — the official Mudra loan portal. Register, fill the application form, and choose from multiple lending institutions. You can compare rates and submit to multiple lenders simultaneously.
  3. 3
    Or Visit Any PSU Bank Branch DirectlyWalk into any branch of SBI, Bank of Baroda, Punjab National Bank, Canara Bank, or any other public sector bank. Ask specifically for "PM Mudra Yojana loan application." The branch manager cannot refuse to give you an application form.
  4. 4
    Submit Application with DocumentsSubmit the filled form along with your documents. For Shishu loans, many banks process on the spot. For Kishore and Tarun, expect 7–21 working days for approval after document verification.
  5. 5
    Receive Mudra Card (for Working Capital Loans)If your Mudra loan is for working capital (not a term loan), you will receive a Mudra RuPay Debit Card — a special card you can use to draw funds as needed, up to your sanctioned limit. This works like an overdraft facility and is unique to Mudra scheme.
  6. 6
    Disbursal and RepaymentFunds are transferred directly to your business account (or personal account for Shishu). Repayment is in monthly EMIs. Pay on time — a clean repayment record makes you eligible for Tarun Plus category in your next loan cycle.
📌 CIBIL Score and Mudra Loans: For Shishu loans up to ₹50,000, banks generally do not require a CIBIL score — this is deliberately designed for first-time borrowers with no credit history. For Kishore and Tarun, a CIBIL score above 650 significantly improves approval chances. If you have no score, start with a Shishu loan and repay cleanly to build credit.
