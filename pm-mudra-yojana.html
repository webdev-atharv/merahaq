Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana
Business Loan Up to ₹20 Lakh — No Collateral

Over ₹27 lakh crore disbursed since 2015. 68% of beneficiaries are women. If you run any small business or trade, a Mudra loan is your right.

What is PM Mudra Yojana?

Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY) was launched on 8 April 2015 to provide institutional credit to micro and small enterprises that lack access to formal banking. It covers non-agricultural income-generating activities — from a vegetable vendor to a small garment unit.

Loans are given through the Micro Units Development & Refinance Agency (MUDRA) via partner banks, microfinance institutions, and NBFCs. No collateral, no guarantor needed.

Four Loan Categories

🌿 Shishu
Up to ₹50,000
For new or very small businesses needing seed capital
📈 Kishore
₹50,001 – ₹5 Lakh
For established businesses needing expansion funds
🏭 Tarun
₹5 Lakh – ₹10 Lakh
For well-established businesses seeking scale-up capital
🚀 Tarun Plus
₹10 Lakh – ₹20 Lakh
For Tarun borrowers with a clean repayment track record

✓ Budget 2024 introduced the new Tarun Plus category, extending Mudra loans up to ₹20 lakh for businesses that have already successfully repaid a Tarun loan.

Who is Eligible?

Any Indian citizen who owns or plans to start a non-agricultural income-generating business can apply. This includes:

  • Street vendors, shopkeepers, and traders
  • Artisans and craftspeople
  • Small manufacturers — food processing, textiles, leather
  • Service providers — salons, repair shops, tutors
  • Transport operators — auto-rickshaw, e-rickshaw owners
  • Women entrepreneurs and Self-Help Group (SHG) members

❌ Agriculture-related activities are not covered under PMMY. However, agri-allied activities like poultry, fishery, bee-keeping, and food processing are eligible.

How to Apply for a Mudra Loan

  1. Visit your nearest bank, RRB, or microfinance institution
    All major banks — SBI, Bank of Baroda, Canara Bank, HDFC, Axis — participate. You can also approach a microfinance institution (MFI) in your area.
  2. Choose your loan category
    Decide between Shishu, Kishore, Tarun, or Tarun Plus based on how much you need and your business stage.
  3. Fill the application form and submit documents
    The form is free. Submit your ID, address proof, business details, photos, and a brief business plan if the bank requires it.
  4. Loan processing — 7 to 30 days
    Shishu loans are typically processed within 7–10 working days. Larger loans may take longer as the bank assesses business viability.
  5. Receive funds and Mudra card
    On approval, you receive a Mudra RuPay card to withdraw working capital as needed up to the sanctioned limit.

✓ Apply online at udyamimitra.in to submit your application to multiple lenders simultaneously without visiting a single branch.

Documents Required

📄 Aadhaar Card 📄 PAN Card 📄 Voter ID / Address Proof 📄 Passport size photos (2) 📄 Business proof (trade licence / GST) 📄 Bank statement – 6 months (Kishore & above) 📄 Business plan / project report (for larger loans)

* Shishu loans often require minimal documentation. Even a verbal explanation of your business may suffice at some MFIs.
